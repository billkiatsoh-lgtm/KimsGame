A tray of objects will be shown for 30 seconds. Study them carefully. Then recall as many as you can in 30 seconds. Wrong guesses deduct points — choose wisely.

Kim's Game originates from Rudyard Kipling's 1901 novel Kim, where the boy hero trains his memory by studying a tray of jewels. It was adopted by the Boy Scouts as a classic training exercise — a staple of scout camps around the world. If you played this as a scout, you'll know how surprisingly tricky it gets!
